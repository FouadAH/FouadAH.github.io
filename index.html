What Remains of Ish



About


“What Remains of Ish” is a single-player 2D action adventure, developed using the Unity game engine, primarily for PC and Mac, with plans to port to consoles. In this hand-drawn adventure, you control a pair of lone siblings as they attempt to flee from the ruins of Ish, once a beautiful city, now a fallen and corrupted land. Fight through creatures that seem to have lost most of their humanity, as you struggle to escape a place that you once called home.

Play our demo on itch.io!

Gaming Academy



About


The Gaming Academy is a series of free and online workshops, organized in collaboration with the American University of Beirut with the express purpose of making game development education accessible to students and aspiring game developer in the MENA-SWANA region. The Gaming Academy gives its participants, both beginners and intermediates, theoretical and practical knowledge that will help push them forward on their game development journey by bringing in professionals from AAA and indie studios to give sessions on development, design, art, storytelling & business.

Over the years we've had the pleasure of collaborating with game development proffesionals from Blizzard, Ubisoft, Vlambeer, Mowhawk Games and many others. The workshops cover a diverse collection of topics, including art, developement, storytelling, music, project management, and pitching.

Check out the website for more info!

Procedural Spider



About


An experiment with procedural animations using Unity's animation rigging package where I created a six-limbed spider equipped with a mounted turret. The spider is capable of climbing steep surfaces while maintaining accurate leg positions and orientation, as well as a walk cycle that is faithful to real-life spider movements.

Spider Survivors



About


Vampire Survivors meets Doom and Control. This project started as an experiement in creating a first-person character controller based on the character mechanics of Controls, but quickly evolved into a tiny roguelike arena-shooter.

Everything in the game from the enemies to the player character is procedurally animated. For the spider AI, I used context sensitive steering behaviors, based on this chapter by Andrew Fray in GameAIPro2
